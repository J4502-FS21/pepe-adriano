Housing: your in-campus options if you are an international student

Text and photo by Jose Luis Adriano

If you are reading this, chances are you're like me — an international student that came to Mizzou to do their Master's or PhD. But it you're a freshman, then I'll point in the right direction: the Residential Life's website, because a whole different set of rules would apply to you.

That being said: finding a place to stay it's going to certainly be among the top priorities to guarantee a happy arrival to Columbia and a smooth transition to your new life.

Grad students coming to Mizzou don’t have a lot of options in housing facilitated by the University. Until Fall 2021, we had two buildings: Manor House and Tara Apartments, but Manor House, a building that the university bought back in 1996, it’s being demolished in June 2021, so they’re not welcoming any more tenants. In fact, they’re kicking out current tenants -mostly international students- once their contract expires. It was a big deal, and the Missourian covered it in detail.

Tara Apartments, on the other hand, are a complex of duplex buildings, walking-ish distance from campus, which offer one-bedroom and two-bedroom apartments. They’re unfurnished, except for the kitchen appliances (stove and fridge), and some might have laundry machines. But since the two-bedroom unit can come in standard and large size, these apartments usually appeal to grad students wanting to move in with their families.

It’s not my intention to replicate Residential Life’s content, where you can access a video tour and consult more about the building amenities. There’s something missing in their website, though. The application process, and the price, that I’ll explain based on my experience.

How to send an application

Applications to Tara Apartments for the Fall semester usually open in March each year. You’ll need to send an email to muhousing@missori.edu, and someone would reply informing you about the options available.

Price for a standard size, two-bedroom unit, was $635 per month as of the 2020-2021 Academic year. And there’s a catch: if you get offered one of the two-bedroom units, you -the person who signs the contract- are responsible for finding a new roommate in case you aren’t coming with your family or partner. This also means that when you do find a roommate, each of you will be paying around $320, which is definitely cheap. It might work for you, but if it doesn’t, there are many off-campus housing options for you.
